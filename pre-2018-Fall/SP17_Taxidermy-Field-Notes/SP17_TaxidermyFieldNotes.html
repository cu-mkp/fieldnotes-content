Taxidermy of 5 rats

May 8, 2017

Chandler 260

Pamela, Naomi, Tianna, Caroline, Ludo, Melinda, Hannah Elmer

  • Alum-wheat starch mixture (Kremer)
  • 1:1 ratio
  • Total = ½ cup (¼ cup each)
  • Thread for sewing incision
  • “J+P Coats Dual Duty Plus”
  •   Cotton covered polyester “for hand sewing.”
  • Rats
  • Sources from Petco on Columbus and 99th Street.
  • “Gourmet Rodent”
  • 2 medium (barcode 63228-19865)
  • 3 large (barcode 63228-19866)
  • Rats A, B, and C are male
  • Rates D and E are female
  • The rats were all washed with Dawn Dish Soap
  • Rat skin is loose and pulls away from interiors.
  • Rat D had lots of fatty tissue
  • Rat A had a big heart, but bigger testicles
  • Rats A, B, C, and E had their eyeballs replaced with glass and plastic beads. Rat D eye’s were left intact and tiny rolls of paper were inserted so as the rat dries the eyes will keep their shapes.
  • Rat A was treated with borax
  • Rat B was treated with borax
  • Rat C was treated with alum and wheat starch
  • Rat D was dried with paper towels inserted into the body cavity and then treated with borax. A film of borax was left behind.
  • Rat E was treated with alum and wheat starch
  • Rat C’s mouth was propped open using a wooden stick on top of the tongue.
  • Rat D’s connective tissue was peeling away from the skin after the borax treatment.
  • The pouch inserted into Rat B was small, so extra linen scraps were added.
  • Rat D had a broken foot which was repaired.
  • Rat B has stuffed linen in the eye socket.
  • Rat D’s ventral was sown, but the break was too big. NJR went over the stitches from the outside as well and taped the foot down.
  • 3 out of 4 of Rat E’s legs were cut. The back left paw has a broken bone.
  • As Rat D was being washed, stuffing came out of its vagina. Borax also came out of its vagina.
  • Melinda noted the borax should be applied like a dry rub, not like breaded chicken.
