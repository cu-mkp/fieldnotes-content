<head>To make a very beautiful and inexpensive golden color</head>

<ab>First of all take a very yellow <m>orange peel</m> and carefully remove the white parts, and pulverize it very well it in a very clean mortar. Take the same amount of <m>sulphur</m>, grind all the ingredients together, pour the mixture into a glass vial, and store it in a cellar or other damp place for eight or ten days. When you want to apply it, you must warm the mixture and apply it wherever you like and you will see a very beautiful color. </ab>

Outline of your Experiment (what will be your workflow, start to finish? ):

  • Procedure:
  • Take 1 bitter orange and remove the peel
  • Dispose of the fruit
  • Place peel on clean flat surface (or hold in hand) with rind facing up
  • Use sharp knife to separate orange peel outer layer from inner pith layer (white soft portion)
  • Take about one quarter of the thin peel and grind in mortar for 5 minutes or until judged to be ‘well pulverized’
  • Estimate by eye the amount of ground peel that has been produced
  • Measure out a comparable amount of sulfur powder
  • Grind the two ingredients together for 5 minute or until well incorporated
  • Use spatula to transfer into glass container
  • Close container with imperfect seal
  • Store container in fume hood
  • (not damp but also don’t want production of carbon monoxide)

MATERIAL 2: Sulfur

Hazard statements from MSDSs for Various Materials:

  • May form combustible dust concentrations in air
  • H315 Causes skin irritation
  • Causes eye irritation
  • Avoid inhalation of dust
  • May be corrosive to metals

Safety Precautions:

  • Use in fume hood (strong odor)
  • Causes skin irritation
  • Wear lab coat, safety goggles, and nitrile gloves
  • Avoid moisture, heat, flame, sparks:
  • when it burns it creates sulfur dioxide which can be fatal if inhaled or explosive if it mixes with oxidizing agents such as chlorates and nitrates
  • when it mixes with water it can create sulfuric acid which is extremely corrosive
  • “Dust is the main concern with solid sulphur so operators must: avoid clouds of dust in the air; exclude all ignition sources; implement regular dust inspections; ensure adequate equipment maintenance.” [1]

Waste Management Plan:

  • Incompatible strong oxidizing agents, Amines, Bases
