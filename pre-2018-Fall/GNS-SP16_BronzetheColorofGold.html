Outline of your Experiment (what will be your workflow, start to finish? ):

  • Based on the comparable recipe from Simone de Monte Dante, I would consider this a modified gilding technique, meaning that it needs much more copper filings than other ingredients (which I consider to be primarily the adhering agents). Therefore I would like to create a proportion of 4:1 copper filings to other ingredients. I am not certain what form of copper I will use yet and thus a certain level of this must be estimated in real time with regards to
  • Procedure:
  • Place stone mortar inside of fume hood
  • Remove all sources of fire or spark
  • Measure out a quarter sized amount of copper powder using small spatula and place in mortar
  • Measure out 1/4th a quarter size (estimated by eye) of yellow ochre, gum arabic, aluminium sulphate
  • Grind ingredients together for 5 minutes, taking note of color change, texture change, heat production, etc.
  • If after 5 minutes the ingredients are not well incorporated and there is no obvious change in color, continue grinding for another 5 minutes
  • Check consistency of mixture with small, flat paintbrush
  • Will the gum make the mixture too thick? Perhaps we will need to suspend in distemper or oil.
  • Transfer mixture to small plastic container
  • Use paintbrush to apply to one square on gessoed panel
  • Check change in color, texture, etc periodically

Variables

  • Vary the source/ purity of copper
  • Vary the types of gum (arabic, tragacanth,
  • Vary the type of alum
  • Could we potentially vary the source of aluminum to organic matter with higher aluminum content
  • Vary dogtooth
  • Vary proportion of ingredients
  • Perhaps we mix ingredients separately: alum and copper and yellow ochre; gum and yellow ochre, then apply gum and place copper mixture on areas where applied.
  • Vary the amount of grinding time
